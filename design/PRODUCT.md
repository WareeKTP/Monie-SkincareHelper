# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Skincare beginners (ages 18–35) who are overwhelmed by the complexity of routines. They're discovering AM/PM skincare for the first time: unsure what actives do, how to layer products safely, or whether their existing products clash.

## Product Purpose
Monie teaches beginners what skincare ingredients do, lets them drag products into an AM/PM routine, and flags ingredient clashes in real time. Success: a user goes from confused to confidently running their first routine, armed with ingredient knowledge and zero anxiety.

## Brand Personality
Warm, encouraging, knowledgeable-friend. Not clinical, not intimidating. The brand feels like a friend who knows the science but explains it in plain words without condescension. Playful characters and warm colours carry the personality.

## Anti-references
- Clinical dermatology sites (cold white, jargon-heavy, intimidating)
- Luxury skincare brands (aspirational, exclusive, cool editorial)
- Dense ingredient-database tools (information overload, no guidance)

## Design Principles
1. **Friendly education first** — every element lowers anxiety, not raises it; jargon always gets a plain-English follow-up.
2. **Show, don't tell** — floating cards, animated helper characters, and colour-coded states make concepts tangible without walls of text.
3. **Routine as ritual** — the planning UI should feel satisfying to build, not like filling out a spreadsheet.
4. **Ingredient transparency** — surface what's inside products and flag what to watch for; users build informed habits, not brand loyalty.
5. **Earn trust through clarity** — every interface moment that could feel medical instead carries a reminder: educational tool, not medical advice.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum contrast throughout. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected: all looping animations collapse to a static frame. Color is always supplemented with text labels — no color-only status indicators. All forms are keyboard accessible.
